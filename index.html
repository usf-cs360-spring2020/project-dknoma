Before I get into an established game, I always check for reviews to get a general idea of what I'm getting myself into. Of course, you have to take reviews with a grain of salt as reviews are going to vary most of the time. This dataset takes about 400k reviews of the best selling gfinal_prototype_1.pngames on Steam so there should be a good amount of reviews with significant amounts of hours played meaning users actually played through a game before reviewing it. Someone with a decent amount of hours in a game is bound to be more objective than someone who put the game down after 1 hour. Of course, the same could be said about players who have an extraordinary amount of hours put into a game (3000+ hrs). These people could be more biased towards the game since they play it so often. Although, these players would also most likely know the game like the back of their hand meaning there's potential that their reviews could show off their deeper understanding and knowledge of the game.

Despite player's best efforts to give honest reviews on the games they love there could be other factors as to why the reviews of a game are one way when it looks like they should be the other way. Reviews may not always be honest. Games can get review bombed which means people spamming negative reviews on a game to lower its rating. Other times, an increase in player base may contribute to a higher number and variation of review quality. These visualizations are aiming to help find anything that may not be obvious from staring at walls of review text.
