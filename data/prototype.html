Prototype

Question

For various genres of games, how do the recommendation and helpful scores compare to the amount of hours put into the game each year. Really popular games should have a good range of reviews from helpful reviews from committed players with many hours to reviews that don't have much substance from players who played for around and hour and quit.

Answer

It doesn't seem that there is much useful information in the helpful scores as the yearly average scores are so low. This looks like it's due to how little that feature is used on most reviews. That being said, people who do play a game for a significant amount of time tend to be the ones who leave reviews the most out of all reviewers. As there is no interactivity you can't tell, but the count of reviews posted at certain years along with the average hours played seems to show the peak playing time of certain games. Namely games like PLAYERUNKNOWN'S BATTLEGROUNDS (PUBG), Grand Theft Auto V (GTAV), MONSTER HUNTER: WORLD, Rocket League, and Rust.

Encoding

The visualization is encoding genre by color, using a scatter plot matrix to show the relationship between various columns of data. Something like shape could have been used to differentiate recommended reviews vs. not recommended reviews, but this would have given the visualization a bit too much noise. Instead recommended and not recommended reviews are given their own columns and rows in the matrix. Perhaps this columns could encode their axis via years instead of counts to help show the yearly comparisons better.

Interactivity

In order to get all the details of the reviews, details on demand via mouse hover will be implemented. Brushing or highlighting could also be used to highlight the reviews of a specific game, genre, or by recommended vs. not recommended. Also, game titles would be in the hover details otherwise there would be too much data on the screen.

If a scatter plot matrix is still used, brushing could be used to highlight the same games within each cell of the matrix
